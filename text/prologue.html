“Ah, Strap Silver, Hollywood he-man turned Washington G-man, to what do I owe the pleasure of a visit to my humble lab?”

Strap walks through the doorway silently, shuts the door and locks it behind him, never taking his eyes off Dr Warnoff. His eyes explore the small lab. There on the long wooden table sit myriad flasks and jars, filled with all manner of colorful chemicals. Some are bubbling atop Bunsen burners, others gently smoking of their own accord. On the far end of the table is a black leather valise, not unlike those carried by a doctor on housecall.

Another table, more like a desk, is off on its own, covered in stacks of handwritten papers, no doubt a record of Dr Warnoff's cruel experiments. But what's that in the corner? It looks like a man-sized metal locker, except it's covered in lights and there's a long handle protruding upwards from the base. Strap's eyes fix on it; the doctor notices.

“I see you have taken an interest in my Infinity Box!” Warnoff says as he begins to slowly walk towards Strap.

“An Infinity Box you say? What's it do? Ah-ah, doctor...”, Strap warns the doctor away from approaching. Warnoff throws up his hands and stops walking. He laughs to himself a bit.

“Why Mr Silver it gives you Infinite power! The power to travel through time! To anywhere...anywhere that has ever existed!” Dr Warnoff throws his arms wide open with this last statement, as if the whole of the universe were within his reach.

Strap is incredulous. “Pardon me, doctor, but I don't see how traveling through time can give any man infinite power.”

Warnoff crosses his arms and looks down in thought, then brings a hand to his chin.

“Mr Silver it is a simple matter of imagination plus ambition. An equation so simple even you must be able to solve it.”

“Watch what you say, Warnoff, or I might just come up with an equation you won't much like: your mouth minus all of its teeth!” Warnoff waves off the threat and continues.

“You see, when a man can travel through time, he can bend the fortunes of the world to his own advantage. One thousand, even one hundred years ago, with my scientific knowledge I could become a king among men!”

“That's where you and I are different doctor; I think I might just grab today's paper and try my luck at yesterday's dog track.”

“Such an obvious choice, Mr Silver, to wish for some...some pocket change! What I'm talking about is something greater! Besides, once you go back in time the future is no longer so cut and dry. Say at this dog track you bump into the wrong person and they just happen to own the winning dog. Well now they're distracted and the winning dog is late to the race. “You eggheads are all the same, Doctor, you think those big brains of yours are going to let you take over the world!”

“We already have, Mr Silver, don't you see? While you 'real men' go about finding reasons to kill one another, we 'eggheads' keep finding ways to make it easier and faster for you to do so. It really is a fantastic trick: we work in the safety of our labs creating the weapons of death and you happily march off to war with them! Why, it's almost laughable when you think about it.”

“You lousy fool! You think you have the upper hand? Well what do you think of these upper hands!”

Strap shows his balled-up fists, as large as California cantaloupes.

“Meet Jack Happy and the Piledriver!”

Dr Warnoff sneers like a dog. “You 'real men', yes, you're always naming your body parts aren't you? Well do it, do your worst with the weapons your God gave you. He too is an 'egghead' isn't he?”

Warnoff stumbles backwards towards the Infinity Box, steadying himself by grabbing onto the Radioactivator switch. The machine begins to hum.

“Fool,” says Warnoff, as he deftly dips the punch and glides a long, thin blade deep into Strap's fully exposed rib cage. Strap collapses silently onto the laboratory floor, his heart punctured by a stiletto wielded in the ice cold hand of a master surgeon.
